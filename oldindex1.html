Head Coach

Tobin Echo-Hawk

Head Coach · Colorado School of Mines Softball

A two-time All-American at the University of Nebraska, Tobin Echo-Hawk was part of the Huskers' national rise in the mid-1990s — batting .379 for her career (2nd all-time at Nebraska), compiling 266 career hits (1st all-time), and scoring a then-school-record 61 runs in 1996. She knows what it means to compete at the highest level, because she did it.

Named Mines' 13th head coach in December 2024, Echo-Hawk brings 13 collegiate seasons and 300 wins to Golden — including four NCAA tournament appearances and four Conference Coach of the Year awards at Portland State, and a school-record 13 conference wins in a single season at UTEP. She is a native of Louisville, Colorado, and she is home.

In 2026, her Orediggers led NCAA Division II in triples (40) and set a program record with 122 stolen bases — the first 100-steal season in Mines history. Kendall Aragon finished as the nation's leader in triples and earned First Team All-RMAC and Gold Glove honors. This is Echo-Hawk's brand: fast, smart, and relentless.

Golden, Colorado  ·  5,675 ft Elevation

12 Miles West of Denver. A Different Gravity.

Golden isn't a suburb — it's a mountain town with Clear Creek whitewater, Coors Brewery, and a main street that goes quiet enough to think. Close to everything. Actually a place.

Washington Avenue

Golden's main street. Independent restaurants, coffee shops, and gear stores — 10 minutes from the athletic complex. A real mountain town, not a college strip.

Clear Creek Canyon

The same creek that runs past your right-field fence runs through downtown. Kayaking, hiking, climbing — or just the view from the bridge after a win.

Mount Zion & The M

The white letter M on the hillside above campus is Golden's landmark — and the beginning of one of Mines' most beloved traditions.

Traditions

Things You'll Only Find at Mines

Mines has been building traditions since 1874. Some are engineering spectacles. Some are physical challenges. All of them will matter to you by the time you graduate.

M Climb

Every incoming student climbs the M on Mount Zion at the start of their first year — and paints it. The Mines Softball team makes the climb together, in hard hats, carrying rocks. It's a small thing that means everything. By senior year, you'll have painted that M four times.

E-Days

Engineering Days. A spring weekend where Mines students compete in engineering challenges — including the legendary Cardboard Boat Race down Clear Creek — and celebrate what it means to think like an engineer. It's part competition, part festival, 100% Mines.

500+

Employers · One Campus · Twice a Year

Career Fair

Twice a year, 500+ companies — from Fortune 500s to national labs to startups — come to Golden. Per student, it's the largest STEM career fair in the country. Orediggers walk out with internships and full-time offers. As an athlete, your discipline makes you stand out.

The Whole Team Makes The Climb

One of 3,100. Part of Something Bigger.

The M Climb isn't just a hike. It's your first day as a Mines student, carrying a rock up a mountain with 1,200 of your classmates. Every Mines athlete does it. They remember it forever.
